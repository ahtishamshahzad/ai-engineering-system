# Knowledge — Product

> Durable, reusable product/domain knowledge. Read only when the task needs it. **Not a tutorial dump.** Every entry carries a trust label; change-prone entries carry the metadata block — see `../README.md`. Starts empty.

## What belongs here

- Domain glossary and business rules that persist across features.
- Recurring product patterns and the reasoning behind them.
- Durable domain constraints (regulatory, workflow) that shape design.

## What does NOT belong here

- One project's brief/personas/research → `../../references/product/` (external material) or `../../projects/current/`.
- Speculative product ideas — those are opinions until validated; label them as such if kept.
- Canonical rules → `../../system/`.

## Quality & source requirements

- Reusable and durable; cite the source (domain expert, spec, regulation). Uncited claims are **unverified notes**.
- Label trust: verified-current / project-convention / opinion / deprecated / unverified. Business rules are often **project-convention** — label accordingly.

## Update & deprecation

- Domain rules change; update the `verified` date, or mark **deprecated** with the successor when a rule is replaced.

## How skills reference this

- Planning skills (`../../skills/requirements-analysis`, `feature-planning`) link here for durable domain rules rather than restating them per task.
